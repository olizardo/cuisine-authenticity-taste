Qualtrics Dataset Codebook & Reference Manual

Cuisine Authenticity, Culinary Tastes, and Social Distinction Study

Author

Omar Lizardo

Published

June 24, 2026

Note

This codebook serves as the definitive reference manual for the raw/complete survey dataset qualtrics.dta (and its identical counterpart genresobjects.dta) used in the study of culinary tastes, authenticity, and social distinction. All pre-calculated composite indices, multiple correspondence analysis (MCA) results, and analytical recodings have been removed to preserve dataset hygiene.

1 Introduction & Dataset Overview

This dataset was collected via an online survey administered to United States respondents using the Qualtrics platform. It contains detailed assessments of cultural tastes, aesthetic preferences, daily practices, cuisine judgments, and socioeconomic background indicators.

Following a hygiene audit, all composite, recoded, and derived variables were eliminated from this dataset. The primary Qualtrics data now contains only primary raw measurements, metadata, and experimental variables, consisting of exactly 1,821 observations (rows) and 522 variables (columns).

2 Data Loading Reference

The dataset is stored as a Stata dataset (.dta format) at [dat/qualtrics.dta](dat/qualtrics.dta).

2.1 Loading in R

To load the dataset in R, use the haven package:

library(haven)
library(here)

# Load the Qualtrics dataset
qualtrics_df <- read_dta(here("dat/qualtrics.dta"))

# Preview dimensions
dim(qualtrics_df)
# [1] 1821  522

2.2 Loading in Python

To load the dataset in Python using Pandas:

import pandas as pd

# Load the Qualtrics dataset
df = pd.read_stata('dat/qualtrics.dta')

# Preview dimensions
print(f"Loaded {df.shape[0]} rows and {df.shape[1]} columns.")
# Loaded 1821 rows and 522 columns.

4 Detailed Survey Blocks & Variable Specifications

4.1 Survey Metadata Block

  • Description: Automatic system variables captured by Qualtrics.
  • Variables:
    • startdate / enddate: Survey start and end timestamps.
    • progress: Survey completion percentage.
    • durationinseconds: Time taken to complete the survey in seconds.
    • finished: Binary indicator (1 = Yes, 0 = No) for survey completion.
    • recordeddate: Timestamp when the response was recorded.
    • responseid: Unique respondent identifier.
    • locationlatitude / locationlongitude: Approximate GPS coordinates.
    • distributionchannel: Survey distribution channel (e.g., anonymous link).
    • userlanguage: Respondent’s language configuration.

4.2 Block 28 (Early Demographics)

  • Description: Basic demographics collected at the beginning of the survey.
  • Variables:
    • sex: Gender identity.
      • 1 = Female
      • 2 = Male
      • 3 = Other
    • age: Year of birth (integer). Note: Survey skips to end if year is greater than 2000.
    • race: Race identification (multiple-choice).
      • 1 = White
      • 2 = Black or African American
      • 3 = American Indian or Alaska Native
      • 4 = Native Hawaiian or Pacific Islander
      • 5 = Hispanic/Latino non-white
      • 6 = Asian
      • 7 = Middle Eastern/North African non-white
      • 8 = Other (write-in)
    • race_8_text: Text box for other race write-in.
    • in_school: “Are you currently in school?” (1 = Yes, 2 = No).
    • educ: “What is the highest degree or level of school you have completed?”
      • 1 = Less than 9th grade
      • 2 = Some high school (no degree)
      • 3 = High school graduate (or equivalent)
      • 4 = Some college (no degree)
      • 5 = Associate’s degree
      • 6 = Bachelor’s degree (BA/BS)
      • 7 = Advanced or graduate degree
    • income: “Please indicate the answer that includes your entire household income before taxes.”
      • 1 = Less than $10,000 to 12 = $150,000 or more, 13 = Don’t Know / Decline to Answer.

4.9 Music Player Experimental Blocks

These blocks gather the respondent’s actual music library statistics (top 10 artists, play counts, and genres) depending on which media player they use.

4.9.1 iTunes block

  • Condition: Displayed if respondent has access to iTunes (how_listen or itunes_sort = Yes).
  • Variables:
    • itunes_sort: “Do you currently have access to your iTunes?” (1 = Yes, 2 = No).
    • itunes_1_1 to itunes_10_3 (3 variables for each of 10 slots):
      • itunes_X_1: Musician/Band name (Text)
      • itunes_X_2: Number of plays (Numeric)
      • itunes_X_3: Genre listed (Text)
    • itunes_rotation1_1_1 to _10_1 (Text entries for top 10 rotation artists) and _rotation2_1 to _10 (Dropdown genre assignments, 1–21 scale where 1 = Big Band/Swing to 21 = Other).

4.9.2 Winamp & MediaMonkey Block

  • Condition: Displayed if respondent has access to Winamp/MediaMonkey and NOT iTunes.
  • Variables:
    • winamp_sort: “Do you currently have access to your Winamp or MediaMonkey?” (1 = Yes, 2 = No).
    • winamp_monkey_1_1 to _10_3 (3 variables for each of 10 slots): Musician/Band, Play Count, and Genre.
    • winamp_rotation1_X_1 and _rotation2_X: Current rotation artists and genres.

4.9.3 Spotify Block

  • Condition: Displayed if respondent has access to Spotify and NOT iTunes or Winamp.
  • Variables:
    • spotify_sort: “Do you currently have access to your Spotify?” (1 = Yes, 2 = No).
    • spotify_phone_comp: “Do you currently have access to Spotify on your computer or phone/tablet?” (1 = Computer, 2 = Phone/tablet).
    • Spotify Computer Variables: spotify_comp1_1_1 to _10_1 (Top 10 artists) and _comp2_1 to _10 (Genre dropdown assignments).
    • Spotify Phone Variables: spotify_phone1_1_1 to _10_1 (Top 10 artists) and _phone2_1 to _10 (Genre dropdown assignments).
    • spotify_rotation1_X and _rotation2_X: General rotation artists and genres.

4.13 Cosmopolitanism Block

  • Opinions (cosmo_1): “On a scale from ‘Strongly disagree’ to ‘Strongly agree’, please indicate how strongly you agree or disagree with each statement.” (1–7 scale + 99).
    • cosmo_1_1: “There are many countries that I would like to visit during my lifetime.”
    • cosmo_1_2: “I appreciate the importance of following tradition.”
    • cosmo_1_4: “I feel very close to the people in my community.”
  • Learning customized cultures (cosmo_2): “If I had the time and resources, I would enjoy learning more about the local culture and customs in [Country]” (1–7 scale + 99).
    • Countries: Italy (1), Bangladesh (4), Saudi Arabia (5), Sudan (6), Slovenia (7), Norway (8), Turkey (9), Germany (10), South Korea (11), Kambezi Republic (fictional attention check, 12), Ecuador (13), Bahari (fictional attention check, 14), Kazirstan (fictional attention check, 15), Guyana (16), Thailand (17), Alvania (fictional attention check, 18).
  • Globalization Views (cosmo_3): “On a scale from ‘Very bad’ to ‘Very good’, please indicate whether you think that globalization is bad or good for each of the following:” (1–7 scale + 99).
    • cosmo_3_1: Consumers like myself.
    • cosmo_3_4: The range of choice in things like TV, film, music, food, etc.
    • cosmo_3_5: My ability to sample different cultures.
    • cosmo_3_6: My ability to learn about cultures other than my own.
    • cosmo_3_7: My access to goods and services outside the US.

4.14 Demographics & Social Networks Block

  • Political Views:
    • social: Political stance on social issues (1 = Very Liberal to 7 = Very Conservative, 99 = Don’t Know).
    • econ: Political stance on economic issues (1 = Very Liberal to 7 = Very Conservative, 99 = Don’t Know).
  • Residency:
    • urban_rural: “Which of the following best describes the area you live in?” (1 = Urban, 2 = Suburban, 3 = Rural).
  • Social Networks (network_1 to _30):
    • “We’d like to know if you know anyone with the following jobs… Please mark if they are an acquaintance, a friend, or a family member.”
    • Checkboxes parsed as multiple indicator columns for each of the 30 occupations (e.g., Lawyer, Doctor, Policy Maker, IT, Manager, Union, Scientist, Mechanic, Teacher, Police, Bookkeeper, Artist, Nurse, Cook, Farmer, Cleaner, Construction, etc.).
  • Cultural Exposure & Parents:
    • self_arts: “How would you rate yourself on engagement with the arts?” (1 = Very Low to 7 = Very High).
    • child_arts: “During your childhood how frequently did your parents or guardians engage you with the arts?” (1 = Not At All to 7 = All The Time).
    • mom_educ / dad_educ: Parents’ completed education level (1 = Less than 9th grade to 7 = Advanced or graduate degree, 99 = Don’t know).
    • mom_arts / dad_arts: “How would you rate your mother’s/father’s engagement of the arts?” (1 = Very Low to 7 = Very High, 99 = Don’t Know).
    • rid / rnid: Unique respondent and panel identifiers.
