Data Codebook & Reference Manual

Cuisine Authenticity, Culinary Tastes, and Social Distinction Study

Author

Omar Lizardo

Published

June 24, 2026

Note

This codebook serves as the definitive reference manual for the cleaned dataset cultdat.dta used in the study of culinary tastes, authenticity, and social distinction.

1 Introduction & Data Cleaning Audit

This dataset was collected via an online survey administered to United States respondents using the Prolific platform. It contains detailed assessments of cultural tastes, aesthetic preferences, daily practices, cuisine judgments, and socioeconomic background indicators.

1.1 Data Cleaning & Hygiene Audit

Following a rigorous hygiene audit, several columns that were merely derivatives of other variables or the products of previous statistical analyses (e.g., pre-calculated index averages, latent classes, and redundant reverse-codings) were systematically eliminated from the primary data file. This ensures that the dataset contains only primary raw measurements and prevents data duplication.

The following 12 derived variables were eliminated from the dataset:

Variable Name Stata Label Type of Derivative Original Sources / Components
photo_lca_class LCA Classes from R Previous statistical model output Latent Class Analysis (LCA) classes fitted on landscape:snake
home_count Foodies Aggregate summary count Number of cuisines rated as “Traditional Elder” (1–2)
chef_count Foodies Aggregate summary count Number of cuisines rated as “Professional Chef” (6–7)
r_hood_city_town Reverse-coded Cosmo Direct linear transformation 8 - hood_city_town

r_nation Reverse-coded Cosmo Direct linear transformation 8 - nation
cosmopolitanism Cosmo Index Multi-item average index Mean of hood_city_town, state_region, nation, continent, global_citizen
poli Econ + Social/2 Multi-item average index Mean of social and economic political ideology

Tip

To support downstream analyses that rely on these index values (such as poli for political classification), the recoding script [recode.dat.R](recode.dat.R) has been optimized to compute these indices on the fly during the data-loading phase, preserving full compatibility without cluttering the primary dataset.

2 Data Loading Reference

The cleaned dataset is stored as a Stata dataset (.dta format) at [dat/cultdat.dta](dat/cultdat.dta).

2.1 Loading in R

To load the cleaned dataset and apply the optimized recoding workflow in R, use the following code:

library(haven)
library(here)
library(dplyr)

# Source the optimized recoding function
source(here("recode.dat.R"))

# Load and recode the dataset
dat <- recode.dat()

2.2 Loading in Python

To load the cleaned dataset in Python using Pandas:

import pandas as pd

# Load the Stata dataset
df = pd.read_stata('dat/cultdat.dta')

# Preview the dimensions
print(f"Loaded {df.shape[0]} rows and {df.shape[1]} columns.")

4 Detailed Survey Blocks & Variable Specifications

4.1 Art Judgments Block (IsItArt)

  • Question Text: “Is this image art?”
  • Response Scale (1 to 7):
    • 1 = Definitely not
    • 2 = Probably not
    • 3 = Maybe not
    • 4 = May or may not be
    • 5 = Maybe yes
    • 6 = Probably yes
    • 7 = Definitely yes

4.1.1 Items:

  • crayon: Image of a crayon drawing/crayon box.
  • pawpatrok: Image of Paw Patrol characters (Note: variable name contains a typo pawpatrok).
  • rorshach: Image of a Rorschach inkblot.
  • lastsupper: Image of Leonardo da Vinci’s The Last Supper.

4.2 Aesthetic Judgments Block (Photo)

  • Question Text: “PhotoItems: For each of the following items is a photographer more likely to produce a photo that is ‘beautiful,’ ‘interesting,’ ‘meaningless,’ or ‘ugly’?”
  • Response Scale (1 to 4):
    • 1 = Beautiful
    • 2 = Interesting
    • 3 = Meaningless
    • 4 = Ugly
  • R Factor Labels: In [recode.dat.R](recode.dat.R), these are converted to factors with labels c("Be", "In", "Me", "Ug").

4.2.1 20 Photographic Items:

  1. landscape: A landscape
  2. car_crash: A car crash
  3. girl_with_cat: A little girl playing with a cat
  4. pregnant_woman: A pregnant woman
  5. vase: A flower vase
  6. woman_breastfeeding: A woman breastfeeding
  7. metal_structure: A metal structure
  8. homeless_arguing: Homeless men arguing
  9. fruits_vegetables: Fruits and vegetables
  10. sunset_over_sea: A sunset over the sea
  11. weaver_at_loom: A weaver at his loom
  12. folk_dance: A folk dance
  13. rope: A rope
  14. butcher: A butcher
  15. bark_of_tree: The bark of a tree
  16. famous_monument: A famous monument
  17. scrapyard: A scrapyard
  18. first_communion: A first communion
  19. wounded_man: A wounded man
  20. snake: A snake

4.3 Self-Presentation Block (Fashion)

  • Question Text: “From ‘not at all like me’ to ‘very much like me’ how much do each of these terms describe your style of dress?”
  • Response Scale (1 to 5):
    • 1 = Not at all like me
    • 2 = Slightly like me
    • 3 = Somewhat like me
    • 4 = Like me
    • 5 = Very much like me

4.3.1 10 Aesthetic Descriptors:

  • comfortable: Comfortable style
  • casual: Casual style
  • smart: Smart style
  • inexpensive: Inexpensive style
  • sophisticated: Sophisticated style
  • designer_brands: Designer brands style
  • trendy: Trendy style
  • unique: Unique style
  • sporty: Sporty style
  • edgy: Edgy style

4.4 Cultural Practices Block (Practice)

  • Question Text: “How many times in the past 12 months have you engaged in the following activities?”
  • Response Scale (1 to 7):
    • 1 = 0 times/not in the past year
    • 2 = 1 to 2 times in the past year
    • 3 = 3-5 times (every few months or so)
    • 4 = 6-11 times (every other month or more)
    • 5 = 12-23 times (once or twice a month)
    • 6 = 24-51 times (every other week to almost every week)
    • 7 = 52 or more (once a week or more)

4.4.1 21 Practice Activities:

  • museum: Go to a museum
  • art_gallery: Go to an art gallery
  • symphony_orchestra_opera: Go to the symphony, orchestra, or opera
  • gardening: Gardening or planting
  • carnival_fair_amusement_park: Go to a carnival, county fair, or amusement park
  • music_concert_festival: Go to a music concert or festival
  • play_or_musical: Go to a play, musical, or performance in a theater
  • library: Go to the library
  • fancy_restaurant: Go to a fancy restaurant
  • fast_food: Eat fast food
  • go_for_walk: Go for a walk
  • exercise_or_yoga: Go to the gym, to exercise, or do yoga
  • dance_performance: Go to the ballet or a dance performance
  • movie_theater: Go to see a movie in the theater
  • read_novel_poem_or_play: Reading a novel, poem, or play
  • attend_sports: Attend a sports game or match
  • home_auto_repair: Do home or auto repair
  • hiking_camping_boating: Go hiking, camping, or boating
  • historic_site: Visit a historic site or landmark
  • go_to_festival: Go to a cultural, artistic, food, or neighborhood festival
  • Note: The 16th item in the raw survey practice list (Mark “12-23 times”) was an attention-check question and is excluded from statistical analysis.

4.5 Bourdieu Food Block (Types of Food)

  • Question Text: “On a scale from ‘extremely dislike’ to ‘extremely like’ how much do you dislike or like food that is / [Item]”
  • Response Scale (1 to 7):
    • 1 = Extremely Dislike (Stata: Very Much Dislike)
    • 2 = Very Much Dislike (Stata: Dislike)
    • 3 = Somewhat Dislike
    • 4 = Neither Like Nor Dislike
    • 5 = Somewhat Like
    • 6 = Very Much Like (Stata: Like)
    • 7 = Extremely Like (Stata: Very Much Like)

4.5.1 Items:

  • light_airy_fresh_delicate (Renamed to light): “Light, airy, fresh, delicate”
  • rich_hearty_filling_savory (Renamed to rich): “Rich, hearty, filling, savory”
  • exotic_authentic (Renamed to authentic): “Exotic and authentic”
  • conventional_familiar (Renamed to familiar): “Conventional and familiar”
  • too_large_portion (Renamed to big): “Too large of a portion”
  • too_small_portion (Renamed to small): “Too small of a portion”
  • caviar: Caviar
  • oysters: Oysters
  • chicken_nuggets (Renamed to nuggets): Chicken Nuggets
  • cheeseburger: Cheeseburger
  • sourdough: Sourdough Bread
  • avocado: Avocado

4.6 Foodies Cuisine Authenticity Block (Cuisine Authenticity)

  • Question Text: “Is each type of cuisine below best prepared using ‘a traditional family recipe cooked by an elder at home’ or ‘a developed recipe by a professional chef cooked at a high-end restaurant’?”
  • Response Scale (1 to 7):
    • 1 = A Traditional Family Recipe Cooked by an Elder at Home
    • 2 = Lean more to this side
    • 3 = Lean somewhat to this side
    • 4 = Equal/One not better than the other
    • 5 = Lean somewhat to this side
    • 6 = Lean more to this side
    • 7 = A Developed Recipe by a Professional Chef at a High-End Restaurant

4.6.1 15 Cuisines Rated:

japanese, french, italian, mexican, moroccan, korean, peruvian, native_american (Stata: american Indian/Native American), swedish, pakistani, ethiopian, vietnamese, nigerian, jamaican, lebanese.

4.6.2 Analytical Recoding:

In recode.dat.R, these 15 variables are transformed into factors with .f suffixes: - "Trad" (Traditional Elder): Score < 3 - "Not Sure" (Indifferent): Score 3 to 5 - "High-End" (Professional Chef): Score > 5

4.7 Attitudes & Ideology Block (Fields)

  • Response Scale (1 to 7):
    • 1 = Strongly disagree
    • 2 = Disagree

4.7.1 Items & Renamed Formats:

4.11 Demographics Block (Demographics)

4.11.1 Core Background Indicators:

  • gender: Respondent’s gender identity.
    • 1 = Woman

    • 3 = Non-binary / Trans

    • 3 = High school graduate (or equivalent)
    • 4 = Some college (no degree)
    • 5 = Associate’s degree
    • 6 = Bachelor’s degree (BA/BS)
    • 7 = Graduate/Professional degree
    • Analytical Recoding: Grouped into "educ.f" (5 broader levels).
  • parented (Renamed to peduc): Highest level of education completed by either parent. Same scale as educ.
  • child_arts (Renamed to arts): Childhood exposure to the arts. Rated from 1 (Very Low) to 7 (Very High).
  • income: Total annual household income.
    • 1 = Less than $10,000
    • 2 = $10,000 to $19,999
    • 3 = $20,000 to $29,999
    • 4 = $30,000 to $39,999
    • 5 = $40,000 to $49,999
    • 6 = $50,000 to $59,999
    • 7 = $60,000 to $69,999
    • 8 = $70,000 to $79,999
    • 9 = $80,000 to $89,999
    • 10 = $90,000 to $99,999
    • 11 = $100,000 to $149,999
    • 12 = $150,000 or more
    • 13 = Missing / Prefer not to say
    • Analytical Recoding: Grouped into "inc.f" (12 categories) and "inq.f" (income quintiles).
  • social: Social issues political ideology. Scale from 1 (Very Liberal) to 7 (Very Conservative).
  • economic: Economic issues political ideology. Scale from 1 (Very Liberal) to 7 (Very Conservative).
  • urban_rural (Renamed to city): Description of residential location.
    • 1 = Open country
    • 2 = Farm
    • 3 = Town to small city
    • 4 = Medium city
    • 5 = Suburb of a large city
    • 6 = Large city
    • Analytical Recoding: Grouped into "city.f" (Small Town, Midsized Town, Big City).
  • attend_service (Renamed to relig): Frequency of religious service attendance.
    • 1 = Never
    • 2 = Very rarely
    • 3 = Rarely
    • 4 = Sometimes
    • 5 = Somewhat frequently
    • 6 = Very frequently
    • 7 = Always
  • age2 (Renamed to age): Continuous respondent age calculated from birth year (Q3). Grouped into factor "age.f" (8 cohorts).
  • race: Raw multiple choice race categories (re-categorized to "race.f").

4.11.2 Additional Background & Institutional Indicators:

  • zipcode: Five-digit US zip code of residency.
  • countries: Total count of countries visited.
  • bornagain: Born-again Christian status (1 = Yes, 2 = No).
  • working: Employment/work status category.
  • govtorno: Indicates public/government sector (1) vs private/other sector (2) employment.
  • field: Industry field of employment.
  • field_20_text: Open-text entry for write-in occupational fields.
